Link Best Practices

  • The text inside of your link should tell the user what will happen when they click on it
    • Link text should never say something like "click here"
    • Bad: Click here to submit our form
    • Good: You can contact us by submitting a message through our form
  • Test your links to make sure they work, from every page
  • Use the appropriate type of link when adding one to your page

About Navigation

About Links

You will be learning about and creating six types of hyperlinks:

  • absolute
  • relative
  • fragment
  • email
  • telephone
  • relative with fragment

Absolute Hyperlinks

Absolute hyperlinks are used when links are needed to another website. Example: asu.edu.

Relative Hyperlinks

Relative hyperlinks are used to link to pages within your own site, such as the contact page.

E-Mail Hyperlinks

Email hyperlinks launch the user’s default mail program. For example: Email Kevin.

Telephone Number Hyperlinks

You can practice a phone link in the footer, but here’s one as well: (555) 555-5555.

Fragment Hyperlinks

A fragment link jumps to another part of the same page. Example: Jump to Back to Top section.
